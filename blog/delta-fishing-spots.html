5 Delta Fishing Spots You Haven't Tried Yet

📅 June 25, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read 🎣 Fishing

The Sacramento-San Joaquin Delta stretches over 1,000 miles of interconnected waterways — and yet most anglers hit the same half-dozen spots every weekend. The popular launches at Bethel Island, Discovery Bay, and Stockton Marina get packed before sunrise on any decent Saturday. But the Delta rewards those willing to explore. Here are five overlooked spots where the fish are biting and the crowds aren't.

The Delta is like an iceberg. Most people see the same 10% — the popular marinas, the tournament waters. But the other 90% is where the real fishing happens.

1. Fourteen Mile Slough — North Stockton

Fourteen Mile Slough
📍 North Stockton  |  🐟 Largemouth Bass, Catfish, Bluegill  |  🚣 Bank & Boat
This quiet slough runs through north Stockton with several bank access points that most people drive right past. The tule-lined banks hold excellent largemouth bass, especially in spring when they're bedding in the shallows. Early morning topwater action can be outstanding from April through June.
Local tip: Fish the stretch between Benjamin Holt Drive and March Lane during an incoming tide. The moving water pushes baitfish into the tules and bass stack up along the edges.

2. White Slough — South of Lodi

White Slough Wildlife Area
📍 South of Lodi  |  🐟 Stripers, Catfish, Bass  |  🚣 Bank Access
A wildlife area that most people visit for bird watching, but the slough channels hold surprising numbers of striped bass in spring and fall. The catfish bite is reliable year-round from the levee banks, and you'll rarely see another angler.
Local tip: Chicken liver or cut shad fished on the bottom at dusk produces channel cats in the 3-8 pound range consistently through summer.

3. Connection Slough — Between Franks Tract and Bethel Island

Connection Slough
📍 Contra Costa/San Joaquin Border  |  🐟 Largemouth, Smallmouth, Stripers  |  🚣 Boat Only
While everyone hammers Franks Tract proper, the narrow connection sloughs leading in and out get far less pressure. These transition zones act as highways for bass moving between open water and cover. Rip current breaks and dock pilings along Connection Slough are money spots.
Local tip: Throw a Senko or Ned rig along the pilings during a tide change. Bass stage here waiting for bait to get pushed through the current.

4. Disappointment Slough — Terminous

Disappointment Slough
📍 Terminous  |  🐟 Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill  |  🚣 Small Boat & Kayak
Don't let the name fool you — this narrow, winding slough is anything but disappointing. Heavy tule coverage, fallen trees, and dark tannic water create perfect bass habitat. The crappie fishing in winter is some of the best in the Delta, with slabs stacking up around submerged brush piles.
Local tip: Kayak anglers have a huge advantage here. The narrow channels and overhanging cover make it difficult for larger boats but perfect for paddling and pitching jigs into heavy cover.

5. Middle River — Rough and Ready Island

Middle River — Rough and Ready
📍 West Stockton  |  🐟 Stripers, Sturgeon, Catfish  |  🚣 Bank & Boat
The stretch of Middle River near Rough and Ready Island sees spring striper runs that rival the more famous spots downstream. The deep channel and bridge pilings create current breaks where stripers stage. Bank anglers can access several spots along the levee roads.
Local tip: Fish live mudsuckers or fresh shad on a sliding sinker rig during the outgoing tide in March and April. Stripers in the 5-15 pound class are common.

The Bottom Line

The Delta's vastness is its greatest asset for anglers willing to explore. While tournament anglers pound the well-known spots, these overlooked waters consistently produce quality fish with a fraction of the pressure. Pack a lunch, charge your trolling motor, and spend a day exploring. You might just find your new honey hole.

Remember: always check current fishing regulations with the California Department of Fish and Wildlife, respect private property boundaries, and practice catch and release on breeding fish during spawn season.
